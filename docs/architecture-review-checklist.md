# Architecture Review Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist defines the minimum evidence required before a product enters implementation.

## Required inputs

A product proposal must provide:

- business purpose and target users
- domain boundary and system scope
- major components and integration points
- data ownership and lifecycle expectations
- external dependencies and third-party risk
- security and compliance posture
- quality strategy and validation approach

## Review questions

Architecture review must confirm that the proposed system:

1. aligns with business goals and product scope
2. uses a clear domain or service boundary
3. minimizes avoidable coupling
4. supports security and compliance controls by design
5. can be tested, observed, and operated safely
6. can be evolved without reworking the foundation

## Approval standard

Implementation may proceed only when:

- the architecture summary is documented
- decision rationale is recorded
- security impacts are understood
- release and operational concerns are identified
- review feedback is resolved or explicitly accepted
